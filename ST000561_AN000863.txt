#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Metabolomic analysis of tomato seed germination
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Genomic approaches have accelerated the study of the quantitative genetics that
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	underlie phenotypic variation. We have utilized gas
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	chromatography-time-of-flight/mass spectrometry (GC-TOF-MS) metabolite profiling
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to characterize tomato seeds during dry and imbibed stages using a recombinant
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	inbred line (RIL) population of Solanum lycopersicum x Solanum pimpinellifolium.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	In this article we describe, for the first time in tomato, the use of a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	generalized genetical genomics (GGG) model to study metabolite changes in tomato
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	seeds incorporating genetics as well as environmental effects in a single QTL
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	analysis. The GGG design was used to map genetic quantitative trait loci (G
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	QTLs) and environmental changes by genetic-by-environment interactions (G x E
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	QTLs). A significant canonical correlation was found between metabolites and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	seed quality traits, revealing a close link between seed quality phenotypes and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	a specific combination of metabolites. Densely connected metabolites were
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	extracted using graph clustering from correlation networks, and the clusters
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	were evaluated by biochemical-pathway enrichment analysis. The evidence from
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	this study suggests that the number of significant correlations varied among
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	individual metabolites and that the obtained clusters were significantly
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	enriched for metabolites involved in specific biochemical pathways. Thus, the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	methods described here have the potential to reveal regulatory networks that
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	contribute to our understanding of the complex nature of seed quality.

#SAMPLEPREP
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	The extraction method is modified from the method previously described by
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(Roessner et al., 2000). Approximately a bulk of approximately 70-100 seeds
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(30mg) seeds were homogenized in 2 ml tubes with 2 iron balls (2.5mm),
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	pre-cooled in liquid nitrogen. For the homogenization the micro-dismembrator
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(Sartorius) is used at 1500 rpm. 700μl methanol/ chloroform (4:3) was added
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	together with the standard (0.2mg/ml ribitol) and mixed thoroughly. After 10
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	minutes sonication, 200μl MQ was added to the mixture followed by vortexing and
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	centrifuging (5 mins 13,500rpm). Methanol phase was collected in a glass vial.
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	500μl methanol/chloroform was added to the remaining organic phase and kept on
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	ice for 10 minutes. 200μl MQ was added followed by vortexing and centrifuging
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(5 mins 13,500rpm). Again, the methanol phase was collected and mixed with the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	other collected phase. 100μl was dried overnight in a speedvac (35°C Savant
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	SPD121). The GC-TOF-MS method was previously described by (Carreno-Quintero et
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	al., 2012) with some minor modifications. Detector voltage was set at 1600V. Raw
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	data was processed using the chromaTOF software 2.0 (leco instruments) and
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	further processed using the Metalign software (Lommen, 2009), to extract and
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	align the mass signals. A signal-to-noise ratio of 2 was used. The output was
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	further processed by the Metalign Output Transformer (METOT; Plant Research
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	International, Wageningen) and the mass signals that were present in less than 3
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	RILs were discarded. Out of all the mass signals, centrotypes are formed using
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	the MSclust program (Tikunov et al., 2011). This resulted in 160 unique
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	centrotypes (representative masses). The mass spectra of these centrotypes were
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	used for identification by matching to an in-house constructed library, the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	NIST05 (National Institute of Standards and Technology, Gaithersburg, MD, USA;
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	http://www.nist.gov/srd/mslist.htm) and Golm libraries
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(http://csbdb.mpimp-golm.mpg.de/csbdb/gmd/gmd.html)). This identification is
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	based on spectra similarity and comparison with retention indices calculated by
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	using a 3rd order polynomial function (Strehmel et al., 2008).

SP:PROCESSING_METHOD             	For the homogenization the micro-dismembrator (Sartorius) is used at 1500 rpm
